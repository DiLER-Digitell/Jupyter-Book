Digital Literarcy in higher education#

Finding an apropriate definition for digital literacy is unfortunately a non-trivial endeavour (see (Santos and Serpa, 2017; Tang and Chaw, 2016; Shopova 2014)

To keep it simple, we will use the following working definition: “Digital literacy” can be surmised as the ability to independently find, evaluate, communicate, and handle information from digita media (Shopova 2014).

Digital literacy, therfore, encompasses a range of skills that are necessary to participate and navigat in a society that is increasingly drifting into a digital age, such as

  • the ability to use and understand the use cases of new technologies

  • to navigate the internet

  • to evaluate the credibility of online information

  • to participate in digital spaces (n a professional capacity

  • to communicate and express oneself through digital means

Why is that important?#

  • rising complexity of digital infrastructure and dependency on the skills to navigate and evaluate information to participate in in higher educatio due to the gradual transition of universities towards digital infrastructure

  • escpecially in the natural sciences research is entirely dependent on dealing with digital externalities

  • provides a way to circumvent structural barriers and provides access to information

  • shields against dependency on university ressources and misinformation

What can we do about it?#

  • provide insight into the research process

  • educate on the importance of digital literacy

  • provide the means to help students to educate themselves

What tools can we use to support students#

  • courses

  • eductaional tools

  • productivity tools

  • lowering barriers

  • adopting open science standards

  • adhering to the fair principles
